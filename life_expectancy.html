Life expectancy in years is directly dependant on GDP/person , and Current Health Expenditure per person

  • We can now investigate each countries life expectancy and how it has changed over years
  • For this choose a country from the drop and the life expectancy data from World Development Indicators is graphed out
  • There are some countries that do not follow the relative linear increase because of their history. So for example Iraq , Rawanda have seen dramatic decreases in life expectancy during times of war
  • We also see that countries like Nigeria, Libya, Ethopia have different rates of life expectancy change in different eras.
    These can be tied back to political, social changes in the countries that led to an increase GDP and Current Health expenditure. This shows that Life expectancy is a lagging indicator of both of these.
